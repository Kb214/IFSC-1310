The Lexus

Lexus (レクサス, Rekusasu) is the luxury vehicle division of the Japanese automaker Toyota Motor Corporation. The Lexus brand is marketed in more than 90 countries and territories worldwide and is Japan's largest-selling make of premium cars. It has ranked among the 10 largest Japanese global brands in market value.Lexus is headquartered in Nagoya, Japan. Operational centers are located in Brussels, Belgium, and Plano, Texas, United States.

Why should you get one?

You should get a Lexus because its parent company is Toyota and they are known for how reliable their cars are. The Lexus on its own though is a very great car and is built on high quality materials and it shows through their car prices. They build their cars with carbon fiber, steel frames, durable steel components for handling and comfort, and they have their own special trim made out of wood called the Shimamoku trim. It takes 38 days to make and goes through 67 manufacturing phases. They are very fuel efficient and have very great safety features. Lexus values perfection and is overseen by 10 groups of professional craftsmen that pass on their knowledge to technicians. Each year and everyday they are working to innovate their cars to be the best for their customers.

Most Underrated Lexus Car of 2024

The Lexus LC 500 is a luxury sedan that offers both coupe and convertible build options with 2x2 seating and a naturally aspirated 471-hp 5.0 liter V-8 standard engine. The V-6 engine is also offered at your request. It has absolutely amazing handling and steering abilities with minimal body roll, variable-steering, and adaptive rear wheel steering systems. The LC500 is very heavy with the hybrid being heavier. Despite this, the hybrid’s eclectic components are more favorable with the EPA (Environmental Protection Agency) standards. When it comes to fuel economy the hybrid also succeeds with a total of 29 mpg compared to the 18 mpg with the LC500. You’ll save $6,000 of annual fuel cost as it only costs $3.51 for 25 miles. You’ll also get a bigger tank size of 22.2 gallons.

The pricing of this car goes from $99,800 to $107,000 depending on the build you’re getting. You may also get charged extra for upgrades ranging from 1,900 to $6,400.
